How We Work

Understanding Medical Billing

A comprehensive overview of the medical billing lifecycle and how we optimize every step

Medical billing is a payment practice within the United States healthcare system. The process involves the systematic submission and processing of healthcare claims for reimbursement. Once services are provided, the healthcare provider creates a detailed record of the patient's visit, including diagnoses, procedures performed, and any medications prescribed.

1

Patient Registration

Capturing accurate demographic and insurance information at the point of service

2

Insurance Verification

Confirming coverage, benefits, and authorization requirements before services

3

Service Documentation

Thorough recording of all diagnoses, procedures, and treatments provided

4

Medical Coding

Accurate translation of services into standardized CPT, ICD-10, and HCPCS codes

5

Claim Submission

Electronic submission of clean claims to insurance payers

6

Adjudication

Insurance company review and determination of payment

7

Payment Processing

Posting payments and reconciling accounts with EOB documentation

8

Follow-up & Appeals

Managing denials, following up on unpaid claims, and filing appeals when necessary

ICD-10 Readiness & Expertise

The transition to ICD-10 represented a comprehensive undertaking that impacts every facet of the medical billing business. It requires significant changes to clinical documentation, coding processes, and workflows throughout the entire revenue cycle.

KHJ Transcriptions is fully prepared and has successfully navigated this transition, ensuring our customers are well protected from any impact of the change. Our team stays current with all coding updates, regulatory changes, and best practices.
